About this project

This project analyses publicly available data regarding projects and activities executed in the Czech Republic with the aim to foster social integration.

About the dataset

The data has been collected by the Association for Integration and Migration (Sdružení pro integraci a migraci) and registers projects of social integration financially supported by public and private sources. The raw data has been retrieved from the Association for Integration and Migration website and can be accessed here.

According to the Association’s website (in Czech), in order to increase the information’s validity and completeness, a combination of sources and methodologies have been used in the compilation process:

  • Mapping websites from public and private donors from an international and local level that support activities for the integration of migrants in the Czech Republic.
  • Analysing website content and annual reports from integration activities organisers, usually based in the Czech Republic with a prevailing involvement in the area of migration and migrant integration.
  • Searching in central endowments and grants online files and repositories at the European and Czech levels.

    • The data, contained in an Excel spreadsheet, is made of 11 columns and 5870 rows. It contains information on projects started in 1999 to 2020.

    The main focus of this analysis is the relationship between the number of projects funded, the number of funding institutions (donors), and the number of institutions benefiting from the donations. The database does not contain information about sums.

    Projects and Donors

    For this analysis, I only considered projects whose date of commencement sparks between January 2015 to July 2020. From this, we learn that, in the analysed period, the total number of projects funded was 2976 and the number of donors ascended to 73. It is also observed that the top 5 donors funded 83% of the total and that the Ministry of Education, Youth and Sport of the Czech Republic is by far the largest contributor with 42% of the total.

    Recipients

    When considering the institutions favoured with funds, there seems to be a concentration in terms of the number of projects per institution. On average, the number of projects per institution is 2.49, with 21% of the organisations receiving funding for only one project. The top 5 most favoured institutions received funds for 8% of the projects and boast an average of 49.4 projects per organisation. The table below shows the top 5 recipients.

    Focus on the main donor (Ministry of Education, Youth and Sport)

    In the period analysed, the main donor, the Ministry of Education, Youth and Sport (Ministerstvo školství, mládeže a tělovýchovy České republiky) funded 1260 projects. The organisation receiving more donations from the Ministry was InBáze, z.s. (Občanské sdružení Berkat), which ranks second in the top 5 most favoured institutions and which received funding for 11 projects from the Ministry. The average organisation received funding for 2.12 projects.

    Focus on the main recipients

    Who is funding the main recipients?

    In the case of Organizace pro pomoc uprchlíkům, the main donor is the Ministry of Work and Social Affairs with 25 projects, followed by the European Union with 16.
